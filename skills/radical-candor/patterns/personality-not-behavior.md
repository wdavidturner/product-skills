## Criticize Behavior, Not Personality

"You're disorganized" attacks who someone is. "The report was missing three sections" addresses what they did. Personality criticism feels like a verdict; behavior criticism feels like coaching.

**Incorrect (criticizing personality):**

> Manager: "You're really disorganized. You need to get your act together."

The employee hears: "You are fundamentally flawed as a person." There's no clear action to take, and they're now defensive about their character.

**Correct (criticizing specific behavior):**

> Manager: "The last three reports you've submitted have been missing sectionsâ€”last week it was the risk analysis, this week it was the budget breakdown. What's happening, and how can we fix this?"

The employee hears: "Here's a specific problem I can solve." They can address missing sections without feeling like they need to overhaul their personality.

**Why it matters:**

Personality criticism feels permanent and global ("I'm just a disorganized person"). Behavior criticism feels specific and fixable ("I need to add a checklist before submitting"). People can change behaviors; telling them their personality is the problem just makes them feel hopeless or angry.
