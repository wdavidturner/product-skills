## Not Every Business Should Do SEO

Before investing in SEO, validate that an SEO journey exists. If users don't search for your solution or wouldn't convert from a search, SEO is the wrong channel.

**Incorrect (SEO by default):**

> **Startup Planning Meeting:**
>
> CEO: "We just closed our Series A. What's the marketing plan?"
>
> CMO: "We need to invest in SEO. Everyone does SEO. Our competitors have SEO teams."
>
> CEO: "Great, let's hire an SEO person and an agency."
>
> *Six months later:* Traffic is up 40%, but zero leads came from organic search. The SEO investment generated content that doesn't connect to how customers actually find and buy the product.

**Correct (validating the journey first):**

> **Startup Planning Meeting:**
>
> CEO: "Should we invest in SEO?"
>
> CMO: "Let me check. Our product is enterprise security software. How do customers find us now?"
>
> Sales Lead: "They hear about us at conferences, get referred by peers, or see our content on LinkedIn. Then they request a demo, go through a 3-month evaluation, and need sign-off from IT, legal, and procurement."
>
> CMO: "So even if someone Googles 'enterprise security software' and lands on our site, they won't convert from that visit. The buying process is committee-driven."
>
> CEO: "Then let's invest that budget in the events and content that actually drive our pipeline."
