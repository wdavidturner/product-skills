## The Product Must Get Better With Use

"We have great features" isn't accruing benefit. The product must demonstrably improve for *that specific user* the more they use it. Static products, no matter how good, don't create Level 2 engagement.

**Incorrect (features that don't compound):**

> **Fitness App Strategy:**
>
> "Our app has the best workout library, the cleanest UI, and the most detailed exercise instructions. Users love our features. We keep adding more workouts to the library."
>
> *Problem*: A user who's been with you for 2 years has the same experience as a user on day 1. Both see the same workout library. There's no accumulated value.
>
> *Result*: Users leave for the next shiny app because switching costs nothing.

**Correct (benefits that compound over time):**

> **Fitness App Strategy:**
>
> "The more you work out with us, the better your experience becomes:
> - We learn your fitness level and adjust recommendations
> - We track your progress over months and show your improvement
> - We know which exercises you like and which you skip
> - We've built a history of your PRs, measurements, and milestones
> - Your workout plans adapt to what's working for you specifically
>
> After a year, you have a personalized fitness system that knows you. Starting over with a competitor means losing all that context."

**Why it matters:**
